Fire Watch

Code-Compliant Coverage When Systems Go Down

When a building's fire alarm system, sprinkler system, or standpipe is impaired or taken offline for maintenance, New York City fire code requires a fire watch. This is not optional. The FDNY expects a trained person on site conducting continuous rounds, maintaining a written log, and ready to alert the fire department if a hazard is detected.

Blue Core Shield provides certified fire watch personnel who understand these requirements thoroughly. Our guards know the difference between a fire watch log that will pass an FDNY inspection and one that will not. They patrol on strict intervals, document every round, and carry the proper communication equipment to contact emergency services immediately if needed.

We handle short-notice deployments regularly. If your system goes down tonight and you need someone on site by morning, we can make that happen. Our fire watch guards are available around the clock and can be deployed to any borough in the city.

When Fire Watch Is Required

Fire watch is typically needed when fire protection systems are impaired, under maintenance, or not yet installed in a building under construction.

Commercial Buildings

Office towers and commercial spaces with sprinkler or alarm systems temporarily taken offline for repair or testing.

Residential High-Rises

Apartment buildings and condos where fire alarm panels or sprinkler systems need servicing or replacement.

Construction Sites

New construction projects where fire protection systems have not yet been installed or commissioned.

Industrial Facilities

Warehouses, manufacturing plants, and storage facilities during fire system shutdowns or upgrades.

Hotels and Hospitality

Hotels and event venues that need fire watch during renovations or system maintenance windows.

Healthcare Facilities

Hospitals and clinics where fire system downtime requires immediate compliant coverage for patient safety.
