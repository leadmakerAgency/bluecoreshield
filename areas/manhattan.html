Security Services in Manhattan

Our home base and busiest coverage area. Blue Core Shield has guards stationed across every neighborhood in Manhattan, from the southern tip of the Financial District to the residential blocks of Inwood.

OVERVIEW

Manhattan Security Operations

Manhattan is where Blue Core Shield was founded, and it remains the center of our operations. The borough presents a unique mix of security challenges. Midtown's office towers require strict access control and lobby management. The Financial District demands armed officers for banks and trading firms. Chelsea and SoHo have high-end retail that needs loss prevention support. And throughout the island, residential buildings count on doormen and concierge security teams around the clock.

We currently maintain active security programs at properties from Battery Park City up through Harlem, Washington Heights, and Inwood. Our guards know the transit hubs, the building layouts, and the patterns of foot traffic in every neighborhood they cover. That familiarity allows them to identify problems early and respond to incidents quickly.

Whether you manage a corporate office on Park Avenue, a construction project on the far West Side, or a residential tower on the Upper East Side, our team can build a security plan that fits your property and your budget.

NEIGHBORHOODS

Where We Operate in Manhattan

We cover the entire borough. These are some of the neighborhoods and districts where we have the highest concentration of active accounts.

Midtown

Corporate office buildings, hotels, and retail flagships along Fifth Avenue, Madison Avenue, and the blocks surrounding Times Square and Grand Central.

Financial District

Wall Street banks, corporate headquarters, and government buildings in Lower Manhattan. Armed and unarmed officers providing access control and secure floor coverage.

Hudson Yards and Chelsea

New construction, luxury residential towers, and mixed-use developments along the High Line corridor and the far West Side.

SoHo and Tribeca

High-end retail locations, art galleries, loft residential buildings, and boutique hotels in two of Manhattan's most visited neighborhoods.

Upper East Side and Upper West Side

Residential co-ops and condominiums, private schools, medical offices along the hospital corridor on York Avenue, and cultural institutions along Museum Mile.

Harlem and Washington Heights

Residential buildings, retail corridors along 125th Street and Broadway, healthcare facilities, and new construction projects throughout Upper Manhattan.
